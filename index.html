The 10-Week Evolution

Week 1

Character DNA

Extracting clues from the text to build the foundational identity of your character. So your choices are unmistakably grounded and real.

Week 2

Imagination Expansion

Breaking out of the box and leaving safe choices behind. So your read is wildly different from other self tapes.

Week 3

Emotional Layers

Playing the subtext, not just the text. Understanding secondary emotions. So your performance feels nuanced, surprising, and unforgettable.

Week 4

Physical Character Work

The body language of a stranger. Changing your center of gravity. So casting immediately sees the character physically in your self tape.

Week 5

Vocal Rhythm + Texture

Finding the character's unique voice, pacing, and breath. So your dialogue sounds beautifully spontaneous and lived-in.

Week 6

The Unspoken Story

Mastering silence and subtext. What happens when the character isn't speaking. So you command attention and exude power without saying a word.

Week 7

Bold Risk-Taking

Finding the unconventional choice that no one else in their self tape will make. So you firmly grab and hold the casting director's attention.

Week 8

High-Stakes Scenarios

Raising the emotional temperature and fully committing to the character's desires. So you deliver undeniable emotional gravity straight to camera.

Week 9

Spontaneity & Instincts

Trusting your gut and reacting genuinely within the given circumstances. So you can confidently and effortlessly handle difficult redirects.

Week 10 — The Finale

Integration & Performance

Bringing all the elements together for a magnetic, unforgettable performance. So you finish your auditions knowing you submitted an undeniable self tape.

About Corey Ralston

Acting coach, talent manager, advocate for young performers

Corey Ralston has spent over two decades guiding young actors in the TV & Film industry. Corey, himself, was a child actor since the age of seven in L.A.

He then went on to pursue talent management in 2017, where he currently represents kids and young adults at Bohemia Group. Corey's mission is to help as many young actors achieve success as he possibly can.

He founded the resource community Child Actor 101 for parents of youth actors, moderating discussions with a team of Industry Professionals, giving out frank free advice.

His coaching blends professional insight from talent management with practical, kid-friendly techniques that help actors grow quickly and authentically.

Parents and casting directors alike recognize Corey's coaching for its mix of strategic challenge and safe creative space. Breakthroughs aren't rare—they're expected. Many students have moved from uncertainty to callbacks, bookings, and real confidence under Corey's guidance.
